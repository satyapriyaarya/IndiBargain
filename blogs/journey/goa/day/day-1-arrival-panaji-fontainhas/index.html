Day 1

Arrival + Panaji & Fontainhas Walk

2026-12-01 Part 1 / 6

Arrive at Goa International Airport (Dabolim) or Mopa Airport in North Goa. Transfer to Panaji or Candolim — Panaji suits those who prioritise heritage and old-town charm, while Candolim gives quicker beach access. Use the first hour to confirm your scooter or car rental since deals are better secured in person at local rental counters rather than through hotel desks.

Once settled, walk the Fontainhas Latin Quarter in late afternoon when golden light plays across the painted facades. This neighbourhood has narrow lanes lined with ochre, indigo, and terracotta Portuguese-style houses with iron-grilled balconies, potted plants trailing over window sills, and tiny Catholic chapels at lane corners. The Chapel of St. Sebastian near the main crossroads is calm and photogenic. Move slowly — the neighbourhood rewards unhurried walkers.

Cross toward the Old Secretariat building near the Mandovi riverfront. The river view from the promenade gives you Goa's scale from the water side. Watch local fishing boats and small ferries move across the channel. The evening breeze here is one of Goa's quieter pleasures before the beach zones take over.

Stop at one of the old Portuguese bakeries on 31st January Road for poee (soft hollow Goan bread), bebinca (dense layered palm dessert), or a simple egg pastry. These bakeries have been operating since the early 20th century with unchanged recipes. Buy a small pack to eat with your morning tea tomorrow.

For dinner, the area near the Church of the Immaculate Conception has several restaurants and cafes. Try Goan fish curry rice, prawn rechad, or a prawn balchao starter. Keep dinner light because tomorrow starts with an early beach sweep and full-day outdoor movement.

Before sleeping, charge all devices, download an offline Goa map, confirm scooter pickup time, and research verified watersports operators at Baga or Candolim. Good operators accept phone bookings before 9 PM for guaranteed morning slots.
