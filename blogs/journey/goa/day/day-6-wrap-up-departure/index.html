Day 6

Departure Day + Budget Wrap

2026-12-06 Part 6 / 6

Use the departure morning efficiently if you have a late checkout or afternoon flight. Final beach visits and quick market runs work best before 10 AM while the air is cooler and stalls are less crowded. Calangute and Anjuna areas have reliable stalls for cashews, dried seafood, spice blends, and fixed-price souvenirs near the main junctions.

For transfer to Dabolim Airport from Panaji or Candolim, allow at least 60-90 minutes including loading time. Pre-book your taxi the night before or use GoaMiles app for metered rides. Traffic on the NH66 corridor toward Vasco da Gama can be unpredictable during peak morning hours (8:30-10:30 AM). Mopa Airport in North Goa is 30-40 minutes from Candolim but 75-90 minutes from Panaji.

Typical per-person budget for a well-planned 6-day Goa trip on a shared basis: accommodation INR 5,000-14,000 total, meals INR 3,000-6,000, local transport including scooter rental INR 2,500-4,500, watersports and activities INR 1,500-3,500, shopping INR 1,000-4,000. Total range: INR 13,000-32,000 per person for a balanced mid-range trip.

Best season for this 6-day format: November to February for ideal weather, active beach shacks, and cultural events. October is quieter and cheaper with similar conditions. Monsoon months (June-September) keep beaches closed but Dudhsagar waterfalls are at peak volume for non-beach-focused trips.

For future trips, consider splitting North and South Goa into two separate 3-day visits. This allows slower pacing, deeper exploration, and access to less-known villages and backwater channels. Goa rewards repeat visitors who move beyond the standard circuit.
